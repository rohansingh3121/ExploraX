Coughing in your elbow may not stop the viral particles from spreading: Study

October 8, 2020

Even before the coronavirus pandemic, people were asked to cough or sneeze into their elbow, when in public. It was said that it prevents the germs from spreading, thus preventing those around you from falling sick. But newer studies suggest otherwise. According to a new study, coughing in your elbow is only helpful when you are wearing clothes with long sleeves. The length of the sleeve (which covers your elbow well) can help form a seal and prevent germs from dispersing.

The study

According to the study published on August 25 in Physics of Fluids, unless you are wearing full sleeves, coughing in your elbow does little to stop viral particles from spreading. Without full sleeves, your elbow doesn't form enough of a seal to stop the airflow, allowing the viral particles to eject through many small gaps in the surrounding air. Likewise, using one or both hands to block sneeze also isn't effective. This leaves ample of small gaps for the germ-filled particles to escape, found researchers. If you are not wearing full sleeves, you can use a handkerchief for better protection.

Sneezing in the elbow is especially harmful, if you use elbow bump instead of a handshake, thinking it to be more hygienic. If you are sneezing in your elbow, it is no different than when you are sneezing in your hands and doing a handshake with someone. During the current situation, it's best to wear a mask (any type) to prevent the particles from travelling. Even if you are using a thin cloth or not so good quality disposable mask, it lets the particles travel only up to half a meter.

While in the absence of cover, the particles can travel up to 3 meters. That is why, people are asked to maintain social distancing even while wearing a mask, for double security. If you are not wearing a mask, or not wearing full sleeves, you can simply use a paper tissue and dispose it off immediately.
