‘Avatar’ sequel: James Cameron gets cracking on underwater stunt sequences with Sigourney Weaver

At a time when the world is still coming to terms with the new normal, director James Cameron has been sailing through with ease and his latest photos from the ‘Avatar’ sets are proof.

Taking to the film’s official social media handle, the director shared photos of one of the film’s leading ladies Sigourney Weaver, getting into her wetsuit and performing her stunts in a pool. With motion-graphics cameras in place and markers in place for the visual effects, the actress put on her best smile and flashed the thumbs up while posing for the click.

"From the set of the Avatar sequels: Sigourney Weaver never shies away from a stunt, not even when it's underwater!" read the photo caption on the Avatar official Twitter account.

With the sequel now set to explore the world underwater, it was revealed that the team thought of an innovative way to prep for the shoot. “To prepare the cast of the Avatar sequels for filming, we took them to Hawaii to experience the underwater world,”Cameron said in a social media post while sharing a photo with Weaver on a boat.

The ‘Avatar’ sets were erected in New Zealand a few weeks ago, where the team resumed work after the COVID-induced break. The delays in filming lead to the film’s release also being pushed back by a year. Cameron reached out fans with a statement announcing the delay in ‘Avatar 2’ and its subsequent sequels.

The release of ‘Avatar 2’ has been moved from December 17, 2021, to December 16, 2022.
